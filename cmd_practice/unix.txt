UNIX is a powerful, multiuser and multitasking operating system that was originally developed at Bell Labs. It is known for its stability, security, and efficiency, which makes it popular in servers, workstations, and large computing systems. UNIX uses a hierarchical file system and provides a rich set of command-line tools that help users perform tasks easily. Many modern operating systems, including Linux and macOS, are based on UNIX concepts. Because of its reliability and strong design, UNIX is widely used in enterprise and scientific environments.
